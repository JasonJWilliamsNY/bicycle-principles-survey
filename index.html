    About The PrinciplesðŸ”—

    Life science is rapidly increasing in interdisciplinarity, making career-spanning learning critical. New methods and deeper research questions requires scientists and educators to traverse widening skill gaps to remain competent. Short-format training (SFT) â€” including workshops, bootcamps, and short courses â€” is a solution many turn to. However, the effectiveness of SFT is lower than many realize. Overall, SFT is delivered without sufficient grounding in evidence-based pedagogy, and systemic inequity limits inclusion of all learners.

    The Bicycle Principles assemble education science and community experience into a framework for improving short-format training (SFT) through two cyclic (hence "bi-cycle") and iterative processes:

    The Bicycle Principles for Short Format TrainingðŸ”—

    Core Principles - apply to all short-format training and are grounded in research and work in education, diversity, equity, and inclusion:

    All Short-format training should...

    1. Use Best Evidence; grounded in evidence-based practices from education research and community consensus.

    2. Promote Catalytic Learning; consider how to prepare learners to implement new knowledge and where appropriate, position learners to be self-directed.

    3. Be Effective; provide evidence to learners that they have made progress in achieving learning goals.

    4. Be Inclusive; maximize the ability of all learners to participate in and benefit from the learning experience.

    Community Principles - apply when short-format training is organized by communities (e.g., by science discipline, institution, or career stage) to achieve their objectives:

    Communities increase the impact of Short-format by working to...

    1. Reach: Take action to reach new types and larger audiences of learners.

    2. Scale: Take action to scale delivery of short-format training by new groups and larger numbers of instructors and instructional producers.

    3. Sustain: Take action to sustain the availability, usability,relevance, and reliability of learning materials and their supporting infrastructures.
